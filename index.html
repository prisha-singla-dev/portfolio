About

Most "AI engineers" can wire an LLM call to a prompt. Fewer can answer the question that actually matters in production: what happens when it's wrong, slow, or too expensive at scale?

That question is the thread through everything I build. Over the last 2.5 years I've shipped multi-agent workflows, NL2SQL and RAG systems, and the cost/reliability infrastructure underneath them — at Capgemini, for 250+ daily users, with real SLAs. My open-source LLM Cost Firewall and CodeSentinel exist because I got tired of watching systems demo well and then fall over in production.

I care about the unglamorous half of AI engineering: failure modes, fallback chains, caching strategy, MTTR. It's the difference between a system that impresses in a demo and one that survives contact with real users.
